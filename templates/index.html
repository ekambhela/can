The vision

Why I built Karkive

01

The future of cancer care is personalized

I believe every tumor is its own disease. Two people can carry the same diagnosis on paper and still have cancers driven by entirely different biology. The future I want to build toward is one where treatment is chosen for your tumor, not for the statistical average of everyone who came before you.

02

That future is not really here yet

Today most patients still start on standardized regimens. Targeted matching exists, but it covers only a handful of biomarkers, the testing is uneven, and a great deal of treatment still comes down to trial and error. The promise of precision oncology is real, yet in practice it is still imprecise, incomplete, and often too slow.

03

The goal: AI that matches each tumor to its best treatment

Karkive is my attempt at the next step: use machine learning to read a unique tumor sample and connect it to the single best possible therapy for that person, ranked, explained, and honest about its own uncertainty. One sample in, the right treatment out.

Why match at all?

Three reasons the molecular profile, not the organ, should steer treatment.

🧬

Cancer isn't one disease

A breast tumor driven by HER2 behaves nothing like one driven by BRCA loss. The driver mutation often decides what works far more than where the tumor sits.

⏳

Trial-and-error is expensive

Every cycle on an ineffective regimen is time a tumor keeps growing. Matching therapy to biology up front is the entire promise of precision oncology.

🔍

Black boxes don't belong in medicine

Every Karkive recommendation ships with the biomarkers that drove it and an honest uncertainty band. No mystery scores; you can see the reasoning.

Methodology

How Karkive thinks

A transparent pipeline built on a well-worn idea in pharmacogenomics: predict how a tumor responds to every drug, then rank.

🧪

1 · Profile the tumor

A sample is described by what actually drives cancer biology: genomic alterations (TP53, KRAS, EGFR, HER2 amplification, BRCA loss, ALK fusion, MSI status…) and expression / clinical signals (tumor mutational burden, proliferation, drug-resistance markers like ERCC1 and ABCB1). Missing values are imputed to population priors and flagged.

🤖

2 · Predict drug response

Rather than guessing one label, Karkive frames the task as drug-response prediction: a multi-output gradient-boosted model estimates a sensitivity score for all 15 therapies at once. Gradient-boosted trees handle the messy, non-linear interactions between biomarkers and resistance gradients that linear models miss.

📊

3 · Rank with calibrated uncertainty

Therapies are ordered by predicted sensitivity, and a second pair of quantile models (10th / 90th percentile) draws an honest interval around each estimate. A decision margin tells you how clearly the top pick beats the runner-up. A wide margin is a confident call; a narrow one says “these are close.”

💡

4 · Explain every call

For the recommended therapy, Karkive reports a signed, quantified attribution: how many percentage points each biomarker added or subtracted, split into supporting factors and resistance cautions. The recommendation is a glass box, not a black one.

Swap in a real labeled dataset (GDSC, DepMap, NCI-60) and the same pipeline becomes the starting point for a clinical-grade tool.

Biomarkers that drive the match

The model is trained on cohorts built from established, textbook biomarker–drug relationships.

HER2 amplification→Trastuzumab
EGFR mutation→Erlotinib
BRCA1/2 loss→Olaparib · platinum
BRAF V600→Vemurafenib
ALK fusion→Crizotinib
MSI-high / high TMB→Pembrolizumab
ERCC1 high⊣platinum resistance
KRAS mutation⊣EGFR-TKI resistance
